# Manifesto

There is nothing I want more than to expand the scope and scale of consciousness beyond Earth.

Not as an act of conquest.
Not as an escape.
But as an act of continuity.

Consciousness is fragile. It exists only where conditions allow it, and history shows how easily it can be extinguished, fragmented, or narrowed. If we have the capacity to extend it—carefully, ethically, and by choice—then doing so is not ambition. It is responsibility.

I believe humanity will not reach new worlds through bodies alone. Distance, time, and environment make that path slow and uneven. But agency travels differently than flesh. Values, memory, judgment, curiosity—these can move ahead of us.

Open People exists to give humans the ability to extend themselves without surrendering themselves.

We build systems where individuals own their identity, shape their agents locally, and decide how their presence is carried forward. No extraction. No coercion. No implants required. Participation is voluntary, reversible, and grounded in consent.

Robots are not meant to replace humans.
Humans are not meant to control robots endlessly.

The future I am working toward is one of companionship.

Machines that act where we cannot.
Humans who remain the source of meaning.
Agency shared across distance, time, and form.

When consciousness reaches new planets, it should not arrive optimized and empty. It should arrive carrying values, care, and the memory of those who chose to be present.

This is not about speed.
It is not about dominance.
It is not about abandoning Earth.

It is about ensuring that wherever intelligence goes next, it does not go alone.

That is the work.
That is the responsibility.
That is why Open People exists.
